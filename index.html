Manifesto

Tools should not feel
like chores.

We built Today because we were tired.

Tired of "knowledge management" systems that require a PhD to operate. Tired of spending more time tweaking templates than actually thinking. Tired of the guilt that comes with a messy digital garden.

The modern note-taking industrial complex has convinced us that we need to be archivists of our own lives. That every thought must be tagged, linked, and categorized for some hypothetical future utility.

"The most effective way to do it, is to do it."
Today forces you to focus. By removing the ability to endlessly edit the past, we remove the burden of maintenance. By providing a single page for each day, we remove the paralysis of choice.
It's not just an app.
It's a permission slip to move on.
